Keywords - Most search engines index just on nouns, not verbs or adjectives (and don't use plurals). To look for all forms of a word use an '*' as a wildcard character, e.g. 'space*' would find 'space', 'spacecraft' , 'spaceflight' etc. That is the wildcard means the search will look for this word and all words starting with this word.

Use plenty of keywords to cut down on erroneous matches, and note that most engines take a space between the words as being an 'OR' operator. So you could end up with lots of references containing a single keyword. To avoid this put a plus sign in front of each word as in '+American +Spacecraft' which would look for pages containing only both words, or else you would get pages refering to Americans or Spacecraft, but not necessarily both. This idea works in most search engines.

The above would find the words American and Spacecraft anywhere in a page, but not necessarily in the same context. So if 'an American was aboard the Russian Spacecraft Soyuz' on a particular page, then it would be selected. To be specific in finding pages on 'American Spacecraft' you may need to search on a phrase, which means enclosing the keywords in double quotes as in "American Spacecraft".

If you wanted to find information on Spacecraft excluding American ones then you can enter '-American' that is enter a minus in front of a keyword to be excluded.
