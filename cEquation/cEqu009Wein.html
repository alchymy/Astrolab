Example: The effective temperature of the Sun is 5778 K. Using Wien's law, this gives a peak emission at a wavelength of about 500 nm in the green portion of the spectrum near the peak sensitivity of the human eye.

Wein's Law - formally, Wien's displacement law states that the spectral radiance of black body radiation per unit wavelength, peaks at the wavelength λmax given by the equation on the first tab, where T is the absolute temperature in kelvin. b is a constant of proportionality called Wien's displacement constant, equal to 2.8977729(17)×10−3 m K.
