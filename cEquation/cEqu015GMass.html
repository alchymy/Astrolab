\[ F_{gravity} = m_s \times centripetal acceleration \]
\[ - \frac{M_R m_S G}{R^2} = m_S \times - \left( \frac{v^2}{R} \right) \]
So: \[ M_R = \frac{Rv^2}{G} \]
Variables / Constants:
  • mS is massof star under consideration
  • MR is the mass of the galaxy interior to an orbit of radius R
  • v is the stars orbital velocity
  • G is the graviational constant

This calculation assumes stars are in circular orbits about the galactic centre and the gravitational force that maintains them in orbit is due to the gravitational attraction of the matter in the Galaxy inside the orbit of those stars. The gravity will be just enough to give the star the centripetal acceleration to maintain a circular orbit. The negative sign on one of the equationsshows the gravity is attractive with acceleration toward the Galaxy centre. The equations on Tab 1 show how we obtain an expression to get MR the mass of the galaxy inside the radius R.
