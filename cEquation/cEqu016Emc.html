Einstein used the CGS system of units (centimeters, grams, seconds, dynes, and ergs), but the formula is independent of the system of units. In the SI system (expressing the ratio E/m in joules per kilogram using the value of c in meters per second): E/m = c2 = (299792458 m/s)2 = 89875517873681764 J/kg (≈ 9.0 × 1016 joules per kilogram). So the energy equivalent of one gram (1/1000 of a kilogram) of mass is equivalent to 89.9 terajoules.
