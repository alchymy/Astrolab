The Frank-Sullivan Equation
 

  • Equation and Calculation
  • Notes



\[ A = N_{ast} \times f_{bt} \]
Variables / Constants:
  • A is the number of technological species that have evolved over the history of the observable universe
  • Nast is the number of habitable planets in a given volume of the universe
  • fbt is the chance of a technological species evolving on one of these planets

The conventional Drake parameters below make up the above two.

  • R* is the average rate of star formation in our galaxy
  • fp is the fraction of those stars that have planets
  • ne is the average number of planets that can potentially support life per star that has planets
  • fe is the fraction of the above that actually go on to develop life at some point
  • fi is the fraction of the above that actually go on to develop intelligent life
  • fc is the fraction of civilizations that develop a technology that releases detectable signs of their existence into space


Example: Using Drake's 1961 figures in the order above 1, 0.2, 1, 1, 1, 0.1

Another alternative modification to the Drake equation.

The Drake Equation, at the top of figure 21, multiplies several factors (fractions) to come up with an estimate of the number of extraterrestrial civilizations in the Milky Way that may currently be transmitting radio signals. The Frank-Sullivan Equation, underneath, eliminates the time factor L and just estimates the number of advanced cvilizations likely to have developed over the history of the observable universe, using the remaining factors.
