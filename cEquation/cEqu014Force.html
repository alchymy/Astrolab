Information:

Speed is just a quantity and has magnitude. Velocity is a vector quantity – is has both direction and a rate or magnitude – any change in either counts as an acceleration.

Force = mass x acceleration

F = m x a.

At the Earth’s surface acceleration and so the force, is experienced due to gravity, that is g, which = 9.8ms^2. So at the Earth’s surface the force on an object of mass m is F = m x g.

The classic equation for the gravitational force experienced between two objects is F = GMm / d^2, where M is the mass of one object and m the mass of the other, with d being the distance between their centers. To find g for the surface of the Earth, if the mass of the Earth is M and the mass of an object on the surface is m, then the two equations can be combined m x g = GMm / d^2 the distance between the objects is now the radius of the Earth, the object mass m can be cancelled out by dividing both side by m, which leaves g = GM / d^2 or g = GM / r^2 where d is now becomes the planets radius r. ‘g’ for the surface of any other planet can now be calculated from its mass and radius.
